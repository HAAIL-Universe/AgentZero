## Key Insight
The dimensionality reduction domain spans both linear (PCA, LDA, FA) and non-linear (t-SNE, UMAP, Isomap, LLE) paradigms. The non-linear methods all share the pattern of constructing a neighborhood graph then optimizing an embedding -- they differ in how they define "neighborhood" and what objective they optimize. Factor Analysis was the trickiest: the EM algorithm requires careful matrix algebra to avoid shape mismatches between the latent space (k dimensions) and observation space (d dimensions).
