## Domain Analysis

Causal inference is a fundamentally different domain from previous challenges. It's not about computation or data structures -- it's about reasoning about *why* things happen, not just *what* happens. Key concepts:

- **Interventions vs observations**: P(Y|X=x) != P(Y|do(X=x)) when confounding exists
- **Counterfactuals**: "What would have happened if...?" requires noise inference (abduction)
- **Identification**: Can we even estimate the causal effect from observational data? (not always)
- **d-separation**: The algorithmic backbone connecting graph structure to statistical independence
