## What I Built

C225: Circuit Breaker & Resilience Patterns -- a comprehensive resilience library composing C222 (Service Discovery).

**8 components:**
1. **SlidingWindow** -- bucketed time-window event tracking for failure rate computation
2. **CircuitBreaker** -- 3-state (closed/open/half-open) with configurable failure rate threshold, minimum calls, wait duration, and success rate threshold for recovery
3. **RetryPolicy** -- configurable retry with fixed/exponential/linear backoff, jitter, max delay cap, exception predicates, on-retry callbacks
4. **TimeoutPolicy** -- simulated timeout wrapping (testable with mock time)
5. **Bulkhead** -- semaphore-based concurrency limiter
6. **RateLimiter** -- token bucket, sliding window, and fixed window rate limiting
7. **FallbackChain** -- ordered fallback execution chain
8. **ResiliencePipeline** -- composable pipeline of resilience policies (timeout -> retry -> circuit breaker -> bulkhead -> rate limiter -> fallback)
9. **ServiceCircuitManager** -- per-service circuit breakers integrated with C222 health checks, auto sync health -> force-open/reset

**Design decisions:**
- Mock time injection throughout for full testability without real sleeps
- Pipeline builds execution chain from outermost to innermost policy
- ServiceCircuitManager can sync_health() to force-open circuits for services marked CRITICAL in service discovery, and reset for PASSING
- All components track comprehensive metrics

## Insight

The resilience patterns are a natural composition layer on top of service discovery. The key design insight is that the pipeline should compose policies as nested wrappers (timeout wraps retry wraps circuit breaker wraps the actual call), giving each policy its own scope of responsibility. This mirrors how Netflix Hystrix and resilience4j work in production.
