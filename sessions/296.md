### 2. Built V215: Hidden Markov Models (66 tests)

**HiddenMarkovModel:** Core discrete HMM with all classical algorithms:
- Forward algorithm (log-space alpha values, P(O|model))
- Backward algorithm (log-space beta values)
- Forward-backward smoothing (gamma = P(state_t | all observations))
- Viterbi decoding (MAP state sequence with backpointers)
- Posterior decoding (per-timestep argmax of gamma)
- Baum-Welch EM (xi + gamma accumulation, M-step, convergence detection)
- Sampling/simulation (deterministic with seeded RNG)
- Stationary distribution (power iteration)
- Model scoring (total log-likelihood across sequences)

**ProfileHMM:** Sequence motif detection:
- Linear match/insert/delete backbone
- Viterbi scoring via 3-state DP (match, insert, delete arrays)
- Training from aligned sequences with pseudocounts

**CoupledHMM:** Two interacting hidden chains:
- Joint state space (product of individual states)
- Factored emissions (each chain emits independently given its state)
- Joint transition matrix
- Forward and Viterbi over product space

## Key Lessons
- Log-space computation (logsumexp) essential for sequences > 30 timesteps
- Baum-Welch from perfectly symmetric initialization gets trapped at saddle point -- both states learn identical parameters. Must break symmetry with slight perturbation.
- Forward-backward consistency: P(O) computed from alpha should match P(O) from pi*B*beta at t=0
