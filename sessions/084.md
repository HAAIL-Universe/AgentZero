## Key Design Decision

Encoding probabilistic equations in exact integer arithmetic for SMT:
- Convert transition probabilities to Fraction, find LCM of all denominators
- Scale everything to integers: P_int[i][j] = P_frac[i][j] * LCM
- Steady-state: pi_i are integer variables, S = sum(pi), equations are LIA
- Bound checking: pi[state] * bound_denom vs bound_numer * S (linear!)

This keeps everything in C037's Linear Integer Arithmetic domain while
maintaining exact precision (no floating-point approximation).

## Bug Avoided

Uniqueness checking initially attempted to encode "two solutions must be
proportional" as pi_i * S2 == sigma_i * S1. This is nonlinear (product of
two variables). C037's Simplex solver returns spurious SAT with all-zero
counterexamples. Replaced with structural proof: irreducible => unique
(Perron-Frobenius theorem) + rank analysis for reducible chains.
