## Bugs Found (4)

### Bug 1: Simplex cycling (pivoting)
- **Category**: Algorithm correctness
- **Pattern**: Not always pivoting after fixing violated basic vars. Violated var stays basic, gets re-violated when fixing another var. Causes oscillation on equality constraints.
- **Fix**: Always pivot after update (swap basic/nonbasic), preventing re-violation.

### Bug 2: NEQ encoding as NOT(EQ)
- **Category**: Composition boundary / theory integration
- **Pattern**: Encoding x!=y as NOT(x==y) creates a disjunctive negation that DPLL(T) can't handle -- simplex can't express "expr != 0" as a single constraint. The theory check blocks the Boolean assignment but the same assignment IS satisfiable with different variable values.
- **Fix**: Encode NEQ as (LT OR GT) at the SAT level, making the disjunction explicit for the Boolean reasoner.
- **New pattern**: Disjunctive negation must be explicit -- never encode disjunctive constraints as negated conjunctive atoms in DPLL(T).

### Bug 3: UF term registration in fresh CC
- **Category**: Composition boundary
- **Pattern**: _check_theory creates fresh CongruenceClosure but didn't properly register UFApp terms and their sub-terms. The CC couldn't reason about function applications it didn't know about.
- **Fix**: Recursive _register_uf_term_in_cc that walks term structure.

### Bug 4: Simplex-CC bridge gap
- **Category**: Cross-theory integration
- **Pattern**: Arithmetic equalities (x==y) handled by simplex weren't propagated to congruence closure. So CC didn't know x==y, meaning f(x)!=f(y) wasn't detected as conflicting.
- **Fix**: After simplex check, scan EQ atoms and merge corresponding CC terms.
- **New pattern**: When multiple theories share variables, equalities must be propagated between theories.

## New Bug Patterns
- **Disjunctive negation in DPLL(T)**: Never encode disjunctive constraints as negated conjunctive atoms. Make the disjunction explicit at the SAT level.
- **Cross-theory equality propagation**: When simplex and CC share variables, equalities must flow from arithmetic to equality reasoning.

The DPLL(T) architecture is elegant -- SAT handles Boolean search, theories handle domain reasoning, and they communicate through lemmas.

The 4 bugs clustered at integration boundaries: simplex algorithm correctness, theory encoding design (NEQ), and cross-component communication (CC registration, simplex-CC bridge).
