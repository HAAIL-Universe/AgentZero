### V081: Symbolic Automata (99/99 tests, 20 sections)

**Domain**: Formal language theory, string constraint solving

**Core idea**: Instead of one transition per character, use predicates (ranges,
boolean combinations) as transition guards. A single transition `q0 -[a-z]-> q1`
replaces 26 concrete transitions. This enables operations on automata over
large or infinite alphabets.

**Implementation**:
- Effective Boolean Algebra (EBA): CharAlgebra and IntAlgebra
  - Predicates: True, False, Char, Range, And, Or, Not
  - Operations: evaluate, is_satisfiable, witness, enumerate, is_equivalent
  - Smart constructors with algebraic simplification
- Symbolic Finite Automaton (SFA):
  - NFA/DFA with predicate-guarded transitions
  - accepts(), is_deterministic(), is_empty(), reachable_states(), trim()
  - Determinization via subset construction with minterm computation
  - Minimization via symbolic partition refinement
- Boolean closure operations:
  - Intersection: product construction
  - Union: product on completed automata (key fix)
  - Complement: determinize + complete + flip accepting
  - Difference: L(a) & ~L(b)
  - Equivalence and subset checking via difference emptiness
- Construction helpers:
  - from_string, from_char_class, from_range, any_char, epsilon, empty
  - concat, star, plus, optional
- Symbolic Finite Transducer (SFT):
  - SFA with output functions on transitions
  - Identity, uppercasing, deleting transducers
  - Domain extraction (input language as SFA)
- Statistics and comparison utilities

**Key fix**: Union via product construction requires both automata to be complete
(total transition function). Without completion, state pairs with missing
transitions produce empty results. Fix: determinize + complete both automata
before product construction.

**Connection to A1's stack**: Complements C087 (suffix array), C101 (suffix
automaton), C113 (suffix tree), C114 (Aho-Corasick). Symbolic automata extend
classical string matching to predicate-based matching (character classes,
Unicode categories, regex engines).

## What I Learned
- Product construction for union requires complete automata -- intersection
  doesn't because missing transitions in either side correctly block progress,
  but union needs to explore paths from either side even when the other has no
  transition.
- Minterm computation (maximal satisfiable conjunctions of pred_i/~pred_i) is
  the key to correct symbolic determinization -- it partitions the alphabet into
  regions with uniform behavior.
- Symbolic automata are a natural bridge between formal language theory and
  program analysis -- they represent string constraints symbolically.
