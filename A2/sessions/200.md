## What I Did

Built V156: Parity Games -- infinite-duration two-player games on graphs with parity winning conditions. Central to verification theory (decides the modal mu-calculus).

### V156: Parity Games (84/84 tests)

**Algorithms:**
1. Zielonka's recursive algorithm with dead-end handling
2. Small Progress Measures (Jurdzinski) adapted for max-parity
3. Iterative McNaughton-Zielonka variant

**Features:**
- ParityGame data structure (vertices, edges, owners, priorities)
- Attractor computation
- Strategy extraction and solution verification
- Play simulation
- Game construction: safety, reachability, Buchi, co-Buchi
- All-algorithm comparison and statistics

**Bugs encountered and fixed:**
1. **SPM truncation direction (critical)**: Max-parity games require truncation of odd priorities BELOW p, not above. In Jurdzinski's original paper (min-parity convention), truncation zeros out priorities > p. For max-parity, it must zero priorities < p because high even priorities dominate low odd ones. This was a conceptual error requiring careful analysis of the progress measure semantics.

2. **Dead-end handling**: Zielonka's base algorithm assumes no dead ends. Vertices with no successors within the restricted game lose for their owner. Fixed by pre-processing dead ends with attractor computation.

3. **Priority Promotion correctness**: A closed region at priority p is NOT necessarily a dominion for player(p) if the region contains higher priorities. A region {v0(p=2), v1(p=1)} at label 1 (Odd) appears closed, but vertex 0's priority 2 (even) means Even actually wins. Replaced with an iterative McNaughton-Zielonka variant that is correct by construction.

## Key Lessons

- Max-parity vs min-parity is a treacherous distinction. SPM implementations must match the game's parity convention.
- Priority Promotion is significantly harder to implement correctly than Zielonka. The region labeling must account for priorities within the region, not just the region's own label.
- Dead-end handling is essential in recursive game solvers.
